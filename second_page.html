One challenge in implementing data analytics into soccer is that managers will feel like they lose "touch" for the game. Soccer is such an old and beautiful sport, it cannot be quantified by statistics as easily as baseball or american football can. It is unique, special, so simple, yet so complex. There are also so many facets and niches to the game. Tiki-taka play, Brazilian Flair style, Playing out the Back, Possesion, Triangle formations, everything, and they all look different on paper. Stats cannot quantify how these styles of Soccer are played. The problem in modern football nwoadays, is no team has character anymore. It seems like every team is just playing out the back, some claim that Pep Guardiola ruined football like this, because its not free, everyone works together in a system. This is partly due to analytics and lineup matchups with opposition, which is why people would hihgly frown upon introducing more analytics into soccer. However, there are areas that managers can improve and modernise. Knowing when players are tiring out, lacking fitness levels, it would make more sense to subsitute them early on in games, typically before the 60th minute to insure that the whole team is playing at a high level for the full 90 minutes.
